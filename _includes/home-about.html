About Me

I am a PhD student in the wonderful Department of Statistics at the University of Auckland. By way of research, I am predominantly interested in Bayesian modeling, statistical computing, and graphics. I have also been increasingly involved with iNZight, an easy to use data visualisation and analysis tool.

A bit of history. I started studying at UoA back in 2010, with the intension of becoming a Marine Biologist. This goal soon deteriorated, however, as I was not-so-slowly—and very much surely—converted to the dark side; I found statistics to be far more enjoyable and significantly more applied than the cram-as-much-knowledge-into-their-heads-as-you-can-in-one-semester approach one gets from biology. From there, without too much consideration, I completed both an Honours and Masters degree in Statistics. It would seem research is something I quite enjoy …

Work. Anyone studying statistics at UoA will soon have to learn R, a task for which my keen interest in programming and computers had me well prepared. Halfway through my Honours year, I was picked up by Professor Chris Wild to join the iNZight development team. Little did I know at the time that I would soon be the main entity responsible for almost every aspect of the program: R packages, GUI, repositories, installation scripts, website, docker containers … the list goes on! We now have a global following, thanks mostly to the Data to Insight MOOC.

Other. Besides that, I quite enjoy reading, watching TV shows, playing computer games (just…one…more…turn… and playing piano (in particular Chopin, and I only wish I had the time and patience to learn some Liszt). I'm also an aspiring wine enthusiast, but I've a long way to go on that front.
